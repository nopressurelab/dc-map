<h1>Citizens &amp; activists kit</h1>
<p class="lede">If a datacenter is being announced near you, or you want to research a prospective project before it lands, this is the practical kit. Methodology, the questions to ask, and the organising template that worked in Aragón. All sources cited.</p>

<h2>Who this is for</h2>
<ul>
<li>Residents of an Aragón municipality where a project is announced or proposed.</li>
<li>Journalists, researchers, or civic groups outside Aragón who want to apply the same methodology to their own region.</li>
<li>Elected officials at the municipal level facing pressure to fast-track a project.</li>
<li>Community lawyers preparing alegaciones or judicial appeals.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Section 1, Methodology: how to research a datacenter project</h2>

<h3>Step 1 - Read the first press release critically</h3>
<p>Extract the exact promoter name (parent company + Spanish subsidiary), site municipality, land size (hectares), stated investment (€), stated capacity (MW), timeline, and any anchor-tenant claim. The absence of a named tenant is itself a signal. Save the original article URL.</p>

<h3>Step 2 - Search the official registries</h3>
<p>Search each of the below with the promoter name, project name, and municipality:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://www.boa.aragon.es/" target="_blank">BOA (Boletín Oficial de Aragón)</a> — for PIGA, DIGA, and Declaración de Interés Autonómico orders.</li>
<li><a href="https://www.boe.es/buscar/" target="_blank">BOE (Boletín Oficial del Estado)</a> — for CHE water concession notices (BOE-B-…) and national-level environmental rulings.</li>
<li><a href="https://www.aragon.es/urbanismo-y-ordenacion-del-territorio/planes-y-proyectos-de-interes-para-aragon-pigas" target="_blank">PIGA registry (Gobierno de Aragón)</a> — for the full PIGA file.</li>
<li><a href="https://iber.chebro.es/consultas/raCriterios.aspx" target="_blank">CHE Comisaría de Aguas</a> — water concession applications, expediente numbers (e.g. 2024-DT-659 for AWS Canal Imperial).</li>
<li><a href="https://www.aragon.es/organismos/instituto-aragones-de-gestion-ambiental-inaga" target="_blank">INAGA</a> — environmental impact declarations (DIA) and integrated environmental authorisations (AAI).</li>
<li><a href="https://sigpac.mapa.gob.es/" target="_blank">SIGPAC + Catastro</a> — the agricultural-parcel and cadastral registries: confirm each plot&apos;s prior use (dryland <em>secano</em> / irrigated <em>regadío</em>) and ownership before it is reclassified to industrial.</li>
<li><a href="https://www.aragon.es/red-natural-de-aragon/red-natura-2000" target="_blank">Red Natura 2000 (Gobierno de Aragón)</a> — check whether the site, or its water/grid corridors, intersects a protected area (ZEC/ZEPA).</li>
<li><a href="https://www.ree.es/es/clientes/generador/acceso-conexion/conoce-el-estado-de-las-solicitudes" target="_blank">REE (Red Eléctrica)</a> — grid connection queue status.</li>
<li>Municipal ayuntamiento website and BOP (Boletín Oficial de la Provincia) for municipal licenses and PGOU modifications.</li>
</ul>

<h3>Step 3 — Verify the anchor-tenant claim</h3>
<p>A datacenter without a named end customer is speculative. Confirm the tenant with:</p>
<ul>
<li>The company&apos;s own investor materials or 10-K (for US operators).</li>
<li>Cushman &amp; Wakefield, JLL, Savills, or CBRE datacenter market reports.</li>
<li>Datacenter Dynamics, Bisnow, S&amp;P Global market intelligence.</li>
<li>Direct question to the operator&apos;s Spain press office.</li>
</ul>
<p>If no answer is given, note it in the record.</p>

<h3>Step 4 — Cross-reference the water and energy story</h3>
<p>Match the announced MW against REE&apos;s published nudo capacity in the area. If the site sits on a nudo with reserved but non-energised capacity, note it, this is the failure mode that stranded Box2Bit Cariñena in January 2026. For water, verify whether the site&apos;s cooling technology is evaporative (peak demand during heatwaves) or closed-loop (peak demand more even). Match against CHE&apos;s drought bulletin for that year.</p>

<h3>Step 5 — Look for procedural shortcuts</h3>
<ul>
<li>Was the project declared PIGA or DIGA? PIGA overrides municipal urbanism.</li>
<li>Did INAGA grant an exemption from the ordinary environmental impact evaluation? For AWS in Aragón the exemption covered 320+ hectares (August 2025).</li>
<li>Was &quot;tramitación de urgencia&quot; invoked (Ley 39/2015 art. 33)?</li>
<li>Has the municipality signed away ICIO (Impuesto sobre Construcciones)? This has been a live dispute for La Muela and Villamayor de Gállego.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Section 2, Citizen kit: what you need to know</h2>

<h3>The six axes of local impact</h3>
<ol>
<li><strong>Land.</strong> How many hectares are being reclassified from &quot;suelo no urbanizable&quot; to industrial? What was the land&apos;s prior use? Who owned it?</li>
<li><strong>Water.</strong> Announced m³/year vs the municipality&apos;s current urban use, plus the cooling-tech choice. Ask: what happens in a CHE-declared drought year?</li>
<li><strong>Electricity.</strong> How does the site&apos;s MW compare to the municipality&apos;s current annual demand, and to the region&apos;s? Is the connection guaranteed or queued at REE?</li>
<li><strong>Local heat.</strong> Peer-reviewed research (ASME 2026) reports datacenter waste heat can raise downwind air temperatures by 0.7–2.2°C up to 500 m from the perimeter. In a hot dry basin like the Ebro valley, this compounds heatwave stress.</li>
<li><strong>Tax.</strong> Datacenters are property-tax and construction-tax opportunities, but PIGA processing has been used to waive the ICIO in Aragón (a live dispute for €87 M between La Muela and Villamayor).</li>
<li><strong>Employment.</strong> Compare the announced job numbers (construction vs operational). Datacenters are heavy on capex, light on permanent jobs, typically 100 to 500 permanent per site regardless of MW.</li>
</ol>

<h3>Common claims and how to test them</h3>
<ul>
<li>&quot;100% renewable energy.&quot; Distinguish <strong>market-based</strong> accounting (matched with renewable PPAs or certificates — the basis for a ~0 emissions claim) from <strong>location-based</strong> (the physical grid the site actually draws, ~108 gCO₂/kWh in 2024, up to ~258 g on the CNMC label). The physical draw is never zero, and adding large new demand can pull up marginal gas generation. Ask which specific facility supplies the site, and on which basis the claim is made. Sources: <a href="https://www.electricitymaps.com/grid-in-review-2025/spain" target="_blank">Electricity Maps</a>, CNMC.</li>
<li>&quot;No significant emissions on site.&quot; Ask for the number and MW of backup diesel generators and their air permit. They run during outages plus routine testing (~50–150 h/yr each); a single 2.5 MW unit emits roughly 1.2 t NOx and 0.05 t PM per 100 test-hours — a local air-quality issue distinct from grid CO₂. Source: <a href="https://insideclimatenews.org/news/12112025/data-center-diesel-generators-noise-pollution/" target="_blank">Inside Climate News</a>.</li>
<li>&quot;Water-free cooling.&quot; Ask for the make-up water figure. All cooling systems require some water.</li>
<li>&quot;X thousand jobs.&quot; Break down construction (temporary, mostly non-local) vs operational (few, high-skill). Compare to municipality population.</li>
<li>&quot;€X billion investment.&quot; Ask how much lands as tax revenue vs as tax exemption. In Aragón, ICIO exemption is a recurring dispute.</li>
<li>&quot;Grid connection secured.&quot; Ask whether the connection is contracted with REE and energised, or reserved and queued.</li>
</ul>

<h3>Land, expropriation &amp; biodiversity</h3>
<p>The mapped campuses with a disclosed footprint already total ≈ 1,149 ha — mostly rural, agricultural land. Because <strong>18 of the 21</strong> mapped projects use the <strong>PIGA</strong> fast-track, which declares them of "general interest," the land can be <strong>compulsorily purchased</strong> — a power previously reserved for transport and energy infrastructure. Landowners in Aragón have reported letters offering compensation with as little as <strong>four days</strong> to respond, on plots families have farmed for generations.</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Check the prior use.</strong> Look each parcel up in <a href="https://sigpac.mapa.gob.es/" target="_blank">SIGPAC</a> + Catastro — dryland (<em>secano</em>) or irrigated (<em>regadío</em>) farmland, and who owned it, before reclassification.</li>
<li><strong>Check the environmental shortcut.</strong> INAGA exempted 320+ ha from the ordinary environmental impact evaluation (August 2025).</li>
<li><strong>Check protected areas.</strong> See whether the site or its water/grid corridors intersect <a href="https://www.aragon.es/red-natural-de-aragon/red-natura-2000" target="_blank">Red Natura 2000</a> (ZEC/ZEPA).</li>
<li><strong>Biodiversity objections</strong> have been filed by <a href="https://www.ansararagon.com/alegaciones-al-piga-de-expansion-de-los-centros-de-datos-de-amazon-aws-en-aragon/" target="_blank">ANSAR and Ecologistas en Acción</a>.</li>
</ul>
<p>Full detail and the footprint chart on the <a href="land.html">Land &amp; biodiversity page</a>.</p>

<h3>Your rights under Spanish law</h3>
<ul>
<li><strong>Public consultation (información pública).</strong> Environmental evaluations (Ley 21/2013) and PIGA processes must open a public information period, typically 30 business days. Alegaciones can be filed by any person or group.</li>
<li><strong>Access to environmental information.</strong> Ley 27/2006 gives you the right to request any environmental document from the administration.</li>
<li><strong>Judicial appeal.</strong> Administrative decisions can be challenged before the Tribunal Superior de Justicia de Aragón (TSJA). Ecologistas en Acción filed the first datacenter-focused appeal in Spain in 2026, represented by <a href="https://fondsambiental.org/" target="_blank">Fons de Defensa Ambiental</a>.</li>
</ul>

<h2>Section 3, How to organise</h2>

<h3>The coalition template that worked in Aragón</h3>
<p>The alegaciones against the Microsoft PIGA (January 2026) were filed jointly by nine groups spanning ecology, neighbourhood, heritage, and specialist water/land defence, this composition is worth copying:</p>
<ul>
<li>Regional ecologist federation (Ecologistas en Acción).</li>
<li>Local naturalist association (ANSAR for Aragón; find your equivalent).</li>
<li>Water-defence specific group (Tu Nube Seca Mi Río; No es sequía es saqueo).</li>
<li>Neighbourhood association from the affected municipality (Asociación Vecinal Montes de Torrero-Venecia).</li>
<li>Heritage-defence association (APUDEPA — Acción Pública para la Defensa del Patrimonio Aragonés).</li>
<li>Landscape-defence platform for the affected comarca (Plataforma en Defensa Estepa Huerva y Sermo; Plataforma en Defensa de los Paisajes de Teruel).</li>
<li>Municipal citizen association (Asociación Ciudadana de Muel; El Juncar es Vida).</li>
</ul>
<p>A diverse coalition signals broad public concern and increases legal standing.</p>

<h3>Practical steps in the first 90 days</h3>
<ol>
<li>Confirm the project details in the official registries (Section 1). Publish a fact sheet.</li>
<li>Request the full environmental file and PIGA documentation via Ley 27/2006. Deadline for response: one month.</li>
<li>Identify the exact BOA publication date, this starts the alegaciones clock.</li>
<li>Contact existing regional coalitions (Ecologistas en Acción Aragón, ANSAR) for co-signature.</li>
<li>Draft alegaciones covering: environmental impact, water, energy, land use, fiscal impact, procedural fairness. File before the deadline. Any citizen can file.</li>
<li>If the alegaciones are dismissed, prepare a contentious-administrative appeal with a specialist environmental law firm (Fons de Defensa Ambiental filed the first Spain DC appeal at TSJA).</li>
<li>Get press coverage. Local titles first (Heraldo de Aragón, elDiario.es Aragón, arainfo, Hoy Aragón), then national (El País, elDiario.es, Público).</li>
</ol>
