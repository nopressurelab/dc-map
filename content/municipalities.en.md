<h1>Affected municipalities</h1>
<p class="lede">Six towns bearing the weight of the Aragón datacenter boom. Population figures give a sense of scale, some of the largest campuses are being built next to towns with only a few thousand residents.</p>

<div class="muni">
<h2>La Puebla de Híjar (Teruel)</h2>
<p class="muni-meta">Population ~935. Comarca Bajo Martín. Host of the largest AWS Aragón campus.</p>
<dl class="muni-stats">
<dt>Investment inflow</dt><dd>€5,000 M</dd>
<dt>Datacenter MW</dt><dd>100 MW (guaranteed)</dd>
<dt>Land committed</dt><dd>70 ha next to N-232; &gt;300 ha total across La Puebla + Azaila</dd>
<dt>Water sought</dt><dd>350,000 m³/yr</dd>
</dl>
<p>The most disproportionate scale in the region. AWS is negotiating water rights with farmers in Híjar, Urrea de Gaén, and La Puebla de Híjar in exchange for partial piping of the Acequia de Gaén, plus a 190,297 m³ storage reservoir alongside the campus. Construction is scheduled for autumn 2027. The campus is AWS&apos;s AI-specialised build in Aragón.</p>

<h2>Climate context, why heat matters here</h2>
<p>All six municipalities sit in the semi-arid Ebro depression (Zaragoza province) or the Bajo Martín zone (Teruel). AEMET normal for Zaragoza airport, the reference station, is an annual mean of about <strong>15.5°C</strong> with July daily maxima averaging around <strong>32°C</strong>. During recent heatwaves, the Ebro valley regularly hits <strong>40 to 42°C</strong>; June 2025 was the hottest June on record across most of Spain, and AEMET has flagged Zaragoza and the whole Ebro depression as a <strong>maximum-risk zone</strong> for heatwave impact in summer 2026.<br><a class="cite" href="https://www.aemet.es/es/serviciosclimaticos/datosclimatologicos/valoresclimatologicos?l=9434&k=arn" target="_blank">Source, AEMET Zaragoza climate normals</a> · <a class="cite" href="https://www.ecoticias.com/cambio-climatico/la-aemet-alerta-de-otra-ola-de-calor-con-temperaturas-de-hasta-42-grados" target="_blank">AEMET summer 2026 warning</a> · <a class="cite" href="https://idearagon.aragon.es/atlas/Clima/info/serie-historica-aemet/temperatura/temperatura-media-mensual-anual--grados-celsius-" target="_blank">Atlas Climático de Aragón</a></p>
<p>Peer-reviewed research shows datacenter waste heat can raise downwind air temperature by <strong>0.7 to 2.2°C</strong> at neighbourhood scale, and evaporative cooling water demand rises with cooling load, meaning the driest heatwaves are also when datacenter water withdrawals from the Ebro basin peak. See the <a href="types.html">primer</a> section &quot;How datacenters affect local temperature&quot;.</p>
