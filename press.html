Headline numbers

€47,000 M, in committed datacenter investment across Aragón as of November 2025.
28 projected sites, 11,237 MW of announced capacity, according to the map compiled by Ecologistas en Acción and No es sequía es saqueo. If all built, more than 9× current annual Aragón electricity demand.
50% of Aragón's 2030 electricity demand, projected to be consumed by datacenters alone. Regional demand itself will nearly quadruple to 40 TWh by 2030.
755,000 m³/yr vs 14 hm³/yr, AWS's stated Aragón water consumption vs the critics' estimate. Ratio ~18×; critics' figure equals ~23% of Zaragoza's urban water use.
~65%, share of Aragón's disclosed per-site MW pipeline that is speculative (no publicly named anchor tenant).
€140 M+, ICIO municipal construction tax exemptions declared for Microsoft. La Muela alone stands to lose €53 M, more than 500 years of its combined property and business tax collections.
Operación Perserte, 6 detained, 12 raids, Guardia Civil corruption investigation opened 3 March 2026 targeting Forestalia (promoter of the €12,048 M Búfalo three-site datacenter project) and a former MITECO senior official.
320+ ha, four municipalities, INAGA exemption granted to AWS from the ordinary Environmental Impact Evaluation in August 2025, on the grounds of "little significant impact".
1 lawsuit, the first datacenter-focused judicial appeal in Spain, filed at the Tribunal Superior de Justicia de Aragón (TSJA) against the AWS PIGA, backed by 8 environmental and civic groups.

Quote-ready lines

Aragón is the largest single-region datacenter build-out announced anywhere in Europe. If completed, it would consume more electricity in 2030 than the whole autonomous community used in 2024.
Approximately two-thirds of the disclosed pipeline lacks a named anchor tenant. Box2Bit's €3.4 billion Cariñena project was stranded before construction; that failure is a preview of the wider risk.
The regional PIGA fast-track lets Aragón override municipal planning and, per critics, waive the ICIO municipal construction tax. In August 2025, INAGA also exempted AWS from the ordinary environmental impact evaluation for 320 hectares, on the grounds of "little significant impact".
The owner of Forestalia, promoter of a €12,048 million three-site speculative datacenter project, was detained by the Guardia Civil in March 2026 in a corruption case involving a former senior official of Spain's Ministry for Ecological Transition. The datacenter project was approved by the Aragón government four months earlier.

Frases citables

Aragón es el mayor despliegue de centros de datos anunciado en una sola región en toda Europa. De completarse, consumiría en 2030 más electricidad de la que consumió toda la comunidad autónoma en 2024.
Aproximadamente dos tercios del pipeline divulgado carecen de cliente principal nombrado. El proyecto de €3.400 millones de Box2Bit en Cariñena quedó varado antes de construirse; ese fracaso anticipa el riesgo mayor.
La vía rápida regional PIGA permite a Aragón anular el planeamiento municipal y, según los críticos, exonerar del ICIO municipal de construcción. En agosto de 2025, INAGA también eximió a AWS de la evaluación de impacto ambiental ordinaria para 320 hectáreas, con el argumento de «afecciones poco significativas».
El propietario de Forestalia, promotora de un proyecto especulativo de tres centros de datos por €12.048 millones, fue detenido por la Guardia Civil en marzo de 2026 en un caso de corrupción que involucra a un ex alto cargo del Ministerio para la Transición Ecológica. El proyecto había sido aprobado por el Gobierno de Aragón cuatro meses antes.
